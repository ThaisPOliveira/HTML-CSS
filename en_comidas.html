TYPICAL FOODS OF SPAIN





Spain's typical foods are among the most appreciated in the world. And, of course, this is not for nothing.
Spanish cuisine has a variety of colors, flavors, and spices that make the recipes delicious with unique aromas.

In the country, its gastronomy is also very differentiated and varies according to each region, which, in turn, has its own customs, but maintains aspects in common.

The use of olive oil, strong spices, bread, and seafood are very common in the preparations, as well as wine, which is consumed almost daily by the Spanish.

Get to know these wonderful delicacies now!





Paella





Practical and versatile. These are the adjectives that define Paella, one of Spain's most traditional foods. Easy to prepare, it is made from rice and has several versions.

The original version is Paella of Valencia, with chicken and rabbit, traditionally cooked over a wood fire. But the recipe that has gained fame outside Spain is Paella Marinera, which takes seafood such as shrimp, octopus, squid, and mussels. Regardless of the type, it is prepared and served in a shallow, wide pan with a handle on each side called a Paella, giving rise to the name of the dish

Patatas Bravas





These are potatoes fried in olive oil, cut in irregular shapes and usually accompanied by salsa brava, a hot sauce. In some regions of Spain this sauce is replaced by alioli, a kind of mayonnaise made with garlic and olive oil. It is common to serve Patatas Bravas as tapas, which are the snacks of Spain.

Gazpacho e Salmorejo





These are two tomato soups, served cold, easily found in various regions of Spain and very popular in Andalusia. Because of their lightness and temperature, they are very popular in the summer. The difference between them is that Salmorejo adds stale bread in its recipe and finishes with pieces of hard-boiled eggs and serrano ham, while Gazpacho has a more liquid texture because it (usually) does not add bread and has more vegetable ingredients like cucumber and bell bell pepper.



Download recipe: Gazpacho e Salmorejo





Tortilla





Tortilla is the typical Spanish food that is rooted in every corner of the country. It is eaten anytime and anywhere, whether at home, in bars, or in restaurants. The tortilla recipe is made with eggs and potatoes and resembles an omelet. The versions with fillings vary according to region, the most common being cheese, ham, bell pepper and sausage



Download recipe: Tortilla





Huevos Rotos





Anyone who thinks that eggs are just a side dish doesn't know about Huevos Rotos, a Spanish food of Madrid origin. The eggs are fried in olive oil and placed on top of a portion of fried potatoes and raw ham. The tip to enjoy this dish is to eat it still hot, spreading the yolk over the other ingredients in the company of a bread roll.



Download recipe: Huevos Rotos





Pulpo à Galega





It is a Spanish dish with more than 400 years of tradition, super traditional in Galícia but with origins in the province of León. Locally called Polbo á Feira, the octopus is cooked whole and served in small pieces, seasoned with olive oil, paprika, and salt. Usually served on a wooden plate and accompanied by boiled potatoes.



Download recipe: Pulpo à Galega





Fabadas





Fabadas are an excellent source of calories, much appreciated on cold days and after physical activity. Typical of the Asturias region, it is based on white beans cooked with various types of sausages and pieces of pork. It takes a long time to prepare and its flavor becomes more refined the next day.



Download recipe: Fabadas





Croquetas





Quite different from the Brazilian croquettes. Spanish croquetas are fried cakes with béchamel sauce and various fillings: cheese, cured ham, chicken, codfish, tuna etc. There is so much variety that there are sweet and vegetarian versions. Croquetas can be sold in portions as well as units. Generally eaten as tapas in bars and restaurants.



Download recipe: Croquetas





Pan con Tomate





Besides being healthy, Pan con tomate, a typical dish from Barcelona, is easy to prepare. All you need is a slice of bread, a ripe tomato, garlic, olive oil, and salt. In the Catalan version (pa amb tomàquet), first rub the garlic on the bread, then cut the tomato in half and rub the inside on the bread, and finally add olive oil and salt to taste. Legend has it that the peasants used the fruit to soften the bread from the day before.



Download recipe: Pan con Tomate





Bocadillos





The bocadillos are the most popular sandwiches in Spanish cuisine, made with baguette-type bread. The filling options are so many that it is hard to resist this delicacy found in practically every market in Spain. There are jamón, tortilla, squid, chorizo, cheese, and vegetarian bocadillos.



Download recipe: Bocadillos





Pimientos de Padrón





Originally from South America, Pimentos Padrón are grown in Galiza, where they have adapted well to the milder climate. A curiosity is that they are harvested unripe, a phase in which they have a greater variety of flavors than when ripe. This famous Spanish tapa is simple to prepare, just fry in very hot olive oil and serve with salt.



Download recipe: Pimientos de Padrón





Sangria





Sangria is a wine-based drink, very light and cold, widely consumed on hot days and at end-of-year parties. Besides red wine, which is the base of the drink, it also contains brandy, fruit, sugar, ice, and others.
