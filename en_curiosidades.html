10 Curiosities about Spain




Revealing some cultural aspects of Spain helped us understand even more about Spanish culture and several customs that make us want to return to the country even more.
Check out this list with 10 curiosities about Spain that will make you even more interested in the country:




1. DAYS OF KINGS




The "Day of Kings" (January 06, national holiday) is more important than Christmas for Spanish children. There are parades around the cities with the Wise Men seen by thousands of children who look forward to their gifts (or coal, if they have behaved badly).




2. NATIONAL ANTHEM WITHOUT LYRICS




The Spanish Anthem is totally instrumental. Spain is part of a select group of four nations where the national anthem has no lyrics at all, the others being Bosnia-Herzegovina, Kosovo and San Marino. The "Royal March" was composed for the Spanish Infantry in 1761, but no longer featured a composition.

Not that the Spanish haven't tried to create lyrics for their national anthem. One of the best-known attempts is the lyrics written by José María Pemán in 1928, used during the dictatorship of Francisco Franco and therefore frowned upon today.




3. LA TOMATINA, THE TOMATO WAR




Tomatina is a Spanish holiday that takes place in the city of Buñol, known as the largest tomato war in the world
There are several versions for the origin of the tradition, but the most reliable one is that it all began in 1945 with a fight during the carnival. By chance, there was a tomato stand at the moment - and thus began the "war".




4. BULLFIGHT




The most controversial tradition of all Spanish culture is the Bullfighting (or Toros Race, as it is known in Spain).




5. RATONCITO PÉREZ




In Brazil we have the childish myth that the Tooth Fairy will pick up the teeth that fell and leave a gift. But in Spain, the visitor is Ratoncito Pérez, custom and character who came to Latin America as well
In Catalonia, Spain's autonomous community, bullfighting has been banned since 2012.




6. OLIVE OIL




Spain is the largest producer of olive oil in the world! 44% of all oil consumed in the world is produced by Spain (one of the main production cities is Monterrubio de la Serena).




7. BALL JABULI




Jabuli ball, which means "celebrating" known for being the worst ball in the cup, for making many curves, was created by sports material manufacturer Adidas. It is made of synthetic material and has eleven tires and no seams, which increases speed




8. LA CASA DE PAPEL




La casa de Papel, a television series that tells the story of eight thieves who lock themselves with hostages in the Spanish Mint, is Spain's biggest production.


Watch on: Netflix



9. LA SIESTA




La siesta is a Spanish custom, taking a break after lunch to get some rest. Normally, la siesta lasts between one and a half and two hours and starts at 2 pm.




10. OS CASTELLS




Castells are human towers usually formed in front of city halls to celebrate certain holidays. The tradition is over 200 years old, and is considered by Unesco as Intangible Heritage of Humanity.
