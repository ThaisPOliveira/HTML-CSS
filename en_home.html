SPAIN






History and the historical context

of the second largest country in the European Union





The Spanish territory has been inhabited since antiquity by different populations, such as Moors, Arabs and Romans, who spread across several kingdoms. The unification of Spain could only be achieved through political and economic agreements that permeated the monarchy in the region. The Church also played a powerful role as the country was unified after the adoption of Catholicism as the official religion.

After unification, Spain became one of the greatest powers in the world, especially due to its protagonist during the Great Navigations.






Government System



Spain is currently in a parliamentary monarchy, governed by the Constitution of 1978. The king's role is to choose the president of government and appoint the other members of the government proposed by the president. The figure of the king is inviolable.
The monarchy is of great influence in the country. It was responsible for maintaining the union between the Spanish regions, with a stable homogenization.
